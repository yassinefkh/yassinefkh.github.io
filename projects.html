Recognition of Spatial Relations in a Linguistic Perspective

2024 – 2025

Study and research project carried out during the first year of the VMI Master's program at Université Paris Cité (2024-2025), supervised by Logan Servant, Camille Kurtz, and Laurent Wendling.

This project focuses on the recognition of spatial relations between objects in images, according to linguistic frames of reference. While recent Vision-Language Models (VLMs) like CLIP have achieved remarkable performance in various tasks, they still struggle to accurately capture spatial reasoning and relative object positions.

The goal is to study and model how spatial relations are described from different linguistic perspectives: relative (egocentric), intrinsic, and absolute references. The project includes the implementation of a neural model for identifying egocentric relations, and the integration of external knowledge (coordinates, depth maps) to improve recognition of exocentric relations.

This exploratory work is based on a newly composed dataset annotated with spatial relations, aiming to bridge the gap between spatial perception and linguistic description in computer vision.

Research Internship – Analysis of Relative Position Descriptors

2023

Research internship carried out at the Laboratoire d’Informatique Paris Descartes (LIPADE), supervised by Camille Kurtz and Laurent Wendling.

The internship focused on the analysis of relative position descriptors in computer vision. The main objective was to study and implement the φ-descriptor, a descriptor designed to encode spatial relations between objects in an image.

Related publication: P. Matsakis, M. Naeem and F. Rahbarnia "Introducing the φ-Descriptor A Most Versatile Relative Position Descriptor", ICPRAM 2015
